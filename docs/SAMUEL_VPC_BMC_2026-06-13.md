## Interpretation

Samuel’s core FoodMatch idea is:

> Dish-first food discovery, not restaurant-first search.

Instead of asking “what restaurant should I go to?”, the user says something like:

> I am craving carbonara / spicy noodles / gluten-free dessert nearby.

FoodMatch then finds:

- the best matching dish,
- nearby,
- personalized to taste and allergies,
- with evidence for why it recommends it,
- including hidden gems that Google, TikTok, and influencers miss.

For restaurants, the BMC angle is:

- help restaurants with one standout dish get discovered,
- sell high-intent leads,
- possibly charge commission or sell insights / premium discovery.

## Product positioning summary

FoodMatch should be framed as:

> The craving-first food discovery app that helps tourists, foodies, and people with dietary needs find the best dish nearby, fast, with evidence.

## Potential investor / accelerator framing

- Problem: people search by restaurants, ratings, and influencers, but cravings are dish-specific and context-specific.
- Pain: users waste time scrolling, distrust reviews, and often end up disappointed.
- Solution: an AI dish graph that matches natural-language cravings to specific dishes and restaurants.
- Differentiator: dish-level ranking, taste personalization, allergy awareness, and evidence-backed recommendations.
- Restaurant value: high-intent discovery for dishes that deserve attention but lack influencer visibility.
