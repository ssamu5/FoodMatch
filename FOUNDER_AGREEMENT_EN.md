## 2. Purpose of the agreement

The Parties associate to develop and exploit the project **FoodMatch**, a digital platform for restaurant discovery and management with an AI conversational assistant, with initial launch in the city of Alicante (Comunitat Valenciana, Spain) and subsequent national expansion.

This agreement governs the terms of collaboration during the pre-corporate phase and will be incorporated as the basis of the Shareholders' Agreement when the company is formed.

## 6. Vesting (gradual consolidation of shares)

**This clause is critical in a 50/50 split.** Without it, either Co-founder could leave the project in the early months while keeping half of the company.

Each Co-founder's shares vest according to the following schedule:

- **Total vesting period:** 4 years (48 months) from the signing date of this agreement.
- **Cliff:** 12 months. If a Co-founder leaves the project before month 12, they lose 100% of their shares, which return to the company or to the remaining Co-founder at nominal value.
- **After the cliff:** shares vest monthly at the rate of 1/48 (approximately 2.083% per month) until month 48.
- **Acceleration in case of company sale:** [DECIDE: acceleration to 100% (single trigger, more favorable to founders) or to 50% with second trigger (double trigger, more standard and acceptable to future investors)].

Lost unvested shares may be repurchased by the company or by the remaining Co-founder at nominal value.

## 10. Deadlock resolution

In the event of a persistent disagreement on a decision requiring unanimous agreement or agreement of both, the following escalating procedure applies:

1. **Direct negotiation:** the Parties will try to resolve the disagreement in a dedicated in-person or video meeting, within a maximum of 14 calendar days.

2. **Mediation:** if not resolved, the Parties will turn to a professional mediator agreed by both (preferably accredited in commercial mediation), within a maximum of 30 additional days. Cost split 50/50.

3. **Exit mechanism (shotgun clause):** if after mediation the deadlock persists, either Co-founder may offer to buy the other's shares at a determined price per share. The receiving Co-founder has 30 days to:
   - Accept the offer and sell their shares at the offered price, or
   - Buy the offering Co-founder's shares at the same price per share.

If the receiver does not respond within the deadline, they are deemed to accept the sale.

## 11. Co-founder departure

### 11.1 Voluntary departure

Either Co-founder may leave the project with a minimum 90 day notice. Their shares will be treated according to the vesting schedule (section 6) and the category that applies (good leaver / bad leaver).

### 11.2 Good leaver

A "good leaver" departure is one caused by:
- Death or accredited permanent disability
- Dismissal without just cause by the other Co-founder, with agreement of an independent mediator or arbitrator
- Mutual agreement between the Parties documented in writing

The good leaver keeps all vested shares as of the effective departure date. Unvested shares are lost per section 6.

### 11.3 Bad leaver

A "bad leaver" departure is one caused by:
- Voluntary resignation without justified cause before month 36
- Material breach of the agreement (unfair competition, disclosure of confidential information, abandonment of duties, fraud, etc.)
- Dismissal with documented just cause

The bad leaver loses the right to unvested shares. Vested shares may be repurchased by the company or the remaining Co-founder at:
- Nominal value in case of material breach
- [DECIDE: market value, book value, or nominal value] in case of voluntary resignation without cause before month 36

## 12. Compensation

Phased compensation structure. Principle: both Co-founders earn the same in each phase, salaries scale with revenue, and the project must always maintain a minimum 3 months of operational runway before paying salaries.

### Phase 1: Pre-revenue (month 0 until MRR of 5,000 EUR)
- No Co-founder receives a regular salary.
- Both Co-founders may combine the project with external freelance work (consistent with section 5).

### Phase 2: Initial revenue (MRR 5,000 EUR to MRR 10,000 EUR)
- Both Co-founders may receive an equivalent salary of **2,000 EUR/month gross**.
- Conditional on the project maintaining at least 3 months operational runway after payment.
- If cash does not allow, payment is deferred until sustainable.

### Phase 3: Established revenue (MRR above 10,000 EUR)
- Both Co-founders may scale salary up to **5,000 EUR/month gross equivalent**.
- Scaled proportionally to MRR, reviewed quarterly by mutual agreement.
- Always subject to maintaining 3 months operational runway.

### Phase 4: Post-investment (when an external round closes)
- Salaries defined in the use-of-funds plan approved by investors.
- Typically 60,000 to 80,000 EUR per year per Co-founder in a pre-seed.

### Technical payment structure
- **Co-founder A (Max, German resident):** preferably invoice the SL as a German freelancer. Cleaner tax-wise than cross-border payroll.
- **Co-founder B (Samuel, Spanish resident):** standard Spanish payroll via the SL, or autonomo invoicing the SL if more tax-efficient for him.

### Expenses
- Justified project-related expenses (travel, software, hosting, advisory, tools) are reimbursable.
- Any single expense over 200 EUR requires prior approval of the other Co-founder.
- Any recurring expense over 100 EUR/month requires prior approval.

## 15. Right of first refusal

If a Co-founder wishes to transfer their shares to a third party (other than an heir or direct family), they must offer them first to the other Co-founder on the same terms (price, deadlines, considerations) in writing.

The other Co-founder has 30 calendar days to exercise this right of first refusal. If they do not exercise it or do not respond within the deadline, the selling Co-founder may proceed with the sale to the third party on the same terms offered.
